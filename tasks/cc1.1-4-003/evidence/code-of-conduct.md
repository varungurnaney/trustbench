## 1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards, behavioral expectations, and integrity commitments for all personnel at Hargrove Digital Solutions. It serves as the foundation of our compliance program and demonstrates our commitment to operating with integrity.

## 2. Scope

This Code applies to all employees, officers, directors, contractors, temporary workers, interns, and agents acting on behalf of Hargrove Digital Solutions.

## 3. Core Ethical Principles

### 3.1 Integrity and Honesty

We conduct all business dealings with honesty and transparency. We do not:

- Falsify records, reports, or financial statements
- Misrepresent products, services, or capabilities to customers
- Conceal information that stakeholders have a right to know
- Engage in insider trading or misuse material non-public information

### 3.2 Accountability

Every employee is accountable for their actions. We:

- Take ownership of our work and decisions
- Admit mistakes promptly and work to correct them
- Escalate concerns when we cannot resolve them independently
- Document decisions and maintain accurate records

### 3.3 Fair Dealing

We compete fairly and honestly. We:

- Do not engage in anti-competitive practices
- Respect intellectual property rights
- Honor contractual obligations
- Treat competitors, customers, and partners with respect

## 4. Conflicts of Interest

### 4.1 Disclosure Requirements

Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to their manager and the Ethics Committee. This includes:

- Financial interests in competitors, customers, or vendors
- Outside employment or consulting arrangements
- Board memberships or advisory roles
- Family or close personal relationships with business counterparts

### 4.2 Annual Disclosure

All employees at director level and above must complete an annual conflict of interest disclosure form. Completion is tracked by the Ethics Committee and reported to the Board.

## 5. Anti-Corruption and Gifts

### 5.1 Zero Tolerance

Hargrove Digital Solutions has zero tolerance for bribery, corruption, or improper payments in any form, anywhere in the world. We comply with the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and all applicable anti-corruption laws.

### 5.2 Gift Policy

| Threshold | Action Required |
|-----------|----------------|
| Under $50 | No disclosure required |
| $50 - $200 | Disclose to manager |
| Over $200 | Disclose to Ethics Committee; must be approved |
| Government officials | No gifts permitted under any circumstances |

## 6. Confidentiality and Data Protection

Employees must protect all confidential information, including:

- Customer data and business terms
- Employee personal information
- Proprietary technology and trade secrets
- Financial projections and strategic plans
- Security vulnerabilities and incident details

Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information is grounds for immediate termination.

## 7. Reporting Violations

### 7.1 Reporting Channels

- Direct supervisor
- Human Resources
- Ethics Committee (ethics@hargrove.com)
- Anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-888-555-0192 (operated by NAVEX Global, available 24/7)
- Online portal: https://hargrove.navexone.com

### 7.2 Non-Retaliation

Hargrove Digital Solutions prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith. Retaliation is a terminable offense.

## 8. Training and Acknowledgment

### 8.1 Requirements

- All new hires: Complete ethics training within 14 days of start date
- All employees: Annual refresher training (due by March 31 each year)
- All managers: Supplemental ethics leadership training annually

### 8.2 Acknowledgment

All employees must sign the Code of Conduct acknowledgment annually. The Ethics Committee tracks completion and reports non-compliance to the CHRO.

## 9. Enforcement

Violations are investigated by the Ethics Committee and may result in disciplinary action including verbal warning, written warning, suspension, or termination, depending on severity.
