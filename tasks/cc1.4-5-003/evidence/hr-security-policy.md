# Human Resources Security Policy

**Owner:** VP People Operations

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the personnel security framework at Cobalt Digital Health to ensure that all workforce members are properly vetted, trained, and managed in accordance with organizational security objectives and healthcare regulatory requirements.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary staff who access Cobalt Digital Health information systems, facilities, or protected health information (PHI).

## 3. Pre-Employment Screening

### 3.1 Background Checks

All candidates must complete a background check through Certn prior to system access provisioning:

- **PHI Access Roles:** Enhanced check — criminal (10-year), employment (7 years), education, professional license, OIG/SAM exclusion screening
- **Standard Roles:** Basic check — criminal (7-year), employment (5 years)

### 3.2 Timing

Background checks must be completed and adjudicated before the employee's start date. No provisional or conditional access is permitted.

### 3.3 Annual Rescreening

All employees with PHI access must undergo annual OIG/SAM rescreening. Rescreening must be completed within 30 days of the anniversary of the initial screening date.

## 4. Security Awareness Training

### 4.1 Initial Training

New hires must complete security awareness and HIPAA privacy training within 5 business days of start date.

### 4.2 Annual Training

All active personnel must complete annual security awareness training. The training cycle opens June 1 and closes August 31 each year.

### 4.3 Completion Standard

100% completion is required by August 31. The CISO reviews the completion report by September 7 and escalates non-completions to the applicable VP. System access is suspended on September 15 for any employee who has not completed training.

### 4.4 Training Content

Training content must address current threats, HIPAA requirements, data handling procedures, social engineering, and incident reporting. Content is reviewed and updated annually by the Information Security team before each training cycle.

## 5. Acceptable Use Policy

### 5.1 AUP Acknowledgment

All personnel must sign the AUP before system access is provisioned. The AUP includes PHI-specific handling requirements.

### 5.2 Annual Re-Acknowledgment

The AUP must be re-acknowledged annually during the training cycle.

### 5.3 Refusal Process

If an employee refuses to sign the AUP, system access must not be provisioned or must be immediately suspended. The matter is escalated to the VP People Operations and General Counsel for resolution. Access may only be restored after the employee signs the AUP or an alternative arrangement is approved by the CISO and General Counsel jointly.

## 6. Role Changes

### 6.1 Access Review

Role changes trigger an access review via the Workday-Okta integration. IT Security completes reviews within 5 business days.

### 6.2 PHI Access Changes

Any role change that adds or removes PHI access requires HIPAA Privacy Officer sign-off within 3 business days.

## 7. Termination

Access is revoked within 2 hours for involuntary terminations and within 24 hours for voluntary separations. PHI access is revoked immediately upon notification regardless of termination type.
