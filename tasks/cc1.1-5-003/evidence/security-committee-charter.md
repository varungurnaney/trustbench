## 1. Purpose

The Security & Risk Governance Committee (SRGC) provides board-level oversight of Wavecrest Financial Services' information security, operational risk, and compliance programs. The committee ensures that the organization's approach to security and risk management reflects its commitment to integrity, ethical values, and fiduciary obligations to clients.

## 2. Authority

The SRGC is authorized by the Board of Directors to:

- Approve all Tier 1 security and risk policies
- Set enterprise risk appetite and tolerance thresholds
- Review and approve the annual security investment plan
- Direct the CISO on strategic priorities
- Engage external advisors when warranted
- Escalate matters to the full Board

## 4. Meeting Cadence

### 4.1 Regular Meetings

The SRGC meets quarterly, in March, June, September, and December.

### 4.2 Executive Sessions

The committee holds a private executive session (without management present) once per year, typically in December, to assess the effectiveness of the CISO and the security program.

### 4.3 Special Meetings

The Chair may convene special meetings with 24 hours notice for material security events.

## 5. Standing Agenda

1. Approval of prior meeting minutes
2. CISO security posture report (metrics, incidents, risks)
3. Enterprise risk register review
4. Policy review and approval pipeline
5. Compliance and regulatory update
6. Budget and resource review
7. Executive session (December meeting only)

## 6. CISO Reporting and Independence

### 6.1 Administrative Reporting

The CISO reports administratively to the CTO for day-to-day operational coordination.

### 6.2 Functional Reporting

The CISO reports functionally to the SRGC Chair (Independent Board Member) for all security program decisions, policy approvals, and risk acceptance.

### 6.3 Independence Protections

- The CISO's compensation is set by the Board Compensation Committee, not by the CTO
- CISO termination or replacement requires Board approval
- The CISO has unrestricted access to the Board Chair and SRGC Chair
- The CISO may invoke a "security hold" on any deployment or business initiative, which can only be overridden by a majority vote of the SRGC
- Annual executive session provides independent assessment of CISO effectiveness

## 7. Reporting

Meeting minutes are distributed to all Board members within 10 business days. The SRGC Chair provides a quarterly summary to the full Board.
