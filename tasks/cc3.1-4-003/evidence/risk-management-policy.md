## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the enterprise risk management framework for Skyline Health Inc. in compliance with SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria CC3.1. Skyline Health provides a cloud-based electronic health records (EHR) platform processing protected health information (PHI).

## 2. Risk Governance

### 2.1 Risk Committee

The Risk Committee meets quarterly:
- Karen Obi, CISO (Chair)
- Derek Palmer, CRO
- Lisa Chang, VP Engineering
- Dr. Michael Torres, Chief Medical Information Officer
- Sandra Blake, General Counsel

### 2.2 Risk Owners

Each risk must have a designated Risk Owner accountable for treatment, monitoring, and reporting.

## 3. Risk Register

### 3.1 Required Fields

Risk ID, description, category, likelihood, impact, rating, classification, treatment strategy, controls, owner, review dates, change history.

### 3.2 Review Cadence

| Classification | Review Frequency |
|---------------|------------------|
| Critical | Monthly |
| High | Quarterly |
| Medium | Semi-annually |
| Low | Annually |

## 4. Risk Rating

5x5 matrix. Classifications: Low (1-4), Medium (5-9), High (10-15), Critical (16-25).

## 5. Risk Appetite

- Information Security: Low appetite. Critical requires Board approval for acceptance.
- HIPAA Compliance: Zero tolerance for known PHI exposure risks.
- Operational: Moderate appetite. Service availability target 99.95%.

## 6. Risk Treatment

- Mitigate: Required for High and Critical unless formally accepted
- Transfer: Insurance or contract (CRO + CFO approval)
- Accept: CISO for High; Board for Critical
- Avoid: Business Unit VP + CRO

## 7. Annual Risk Assessment

Comprehensive assessment annually in Q1. Must cover all risk domains including:
- Information security
- HIPAA / regulatory compliance
- Technology and infrastructure
- Third-party / vendor risk
- Clinical data integrity
- Operational resilience
