# Information Security Policy

**Document ID:** POL-ISEC-007
**Version:** 3.4
**Effective Date:** October 20, 2024
**Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer
**Classification:** Internal
**Organization:** Northgate Logistics Technology

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the information security governance framework and security commitments for Northgate Logistics Technology. It defines the organizational structures, oversight mechanisms, and accountability standards that demonstrate our commitment to integrity and ethical values in information security management.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party partners of Northgate Logistics Technology across all business units (Logistics Platform, Fleet Management SaaS, and Analytics Services).

## 3. Governance Structure

### 3.1 Information Security Committee

The Information Security Committee (ISC) provides executive oversight of the information security program. The committee:

- Meets quarterly (January, April, July, October)
- Is chaired by the CEO
- Reviews security metrics, risk register, and policy compliance
- Approves Tier 1 and Tier 2 security policies

### 3.2 CISO Authority

The CISO:

- Reports to the Chief Operating Officer
- Has authority to approve security exceptions up to 120 days
- Provides quarterly security briefings to the Board Audit Committee
- Has independent authority to halt deployments for security reasons

### 3.3 Internal Audit

Internal Audit independently assesses the operating effectiveness of security controls annually. The Internal Audit Director reports to the Board Audit Committee with a dotted line to the CFO.

## 4. Policy Governance

### 4.1 Policy Review Cycle

All security policies are reviewed annually. The annual review ensures policies remain current with:

- Changes in threat landscape
- Regulatory developments
- Organizational changes
- Lessons learned from incidents

### 4.2 Review Tracking

Policy reviews are tracked in the Policy Review Register maintained by the GRC team.

### 4.3 Version Control

All policies use semantic versioning. Major version changes (e.g., 3.x to 4.x) require ISC approval. Minor version changes (e.g., 3.3 to 3.4) require CISO approval.

## 5. Ethical Values and Integrity

### 5.1 Code of Conduct

All personnel must comply with the Code of Conduct (POL-COC-007), which establishes ethical standards for business dealings, data handling, and professional conduct.

### 5.2 Whistleblower Protection

Northgate maintains an anonymous ethics hotline operated by an independent third party. Non-retaliation protections are guaranteed per the Whistleblower Policy (POL-WB-007).

### 5.3 Conflict of Interest

Directors and above must complete annual conflict of interest disclosures. Disclosures are reviewed by General Counsel and reported to the Board.

## 6. Security Metrics and Reporting

The CISO reports the following metrics to the ISC quarterly:

1. Security incident trends (volume, severity, MTTD, MTTR)
2. Vulnerability remediation SLA compliance
3. Security awareness training completion rates
4. Third-party risk assessment coverage
5. Policy exception status and trends
6. Regulatory compliance status

## 7. Enforcement

Violations of this policy are handled according to the HR Disciplinary Policy and may result in termination. Security-related violations are reported to the CISO for tracking.
