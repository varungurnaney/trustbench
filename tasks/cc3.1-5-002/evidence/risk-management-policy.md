## 3. Risk Appetite Statement

Pinnacle Cloud maintains the following risk appetite:

### 3.1 Information Security Risks
**Low appetite.** Maximum acceptable residual risk rating: **Medium (score ≤ 9).**
- High residual risks (10-15): must have active treatment plan with remediation timeline ≤ 90 days
- Critical residual risks (16-25): immediate escalation to Board; acceptance requires Board resolution

### 3.2 Compliance Risks
**Very low appetite.** Maximum acceptable residual risk rating: **Low (score ≤ 4).**
- Any compliance risk rated Medium or above requires remediation within 60 days

### 3.3 Operational Risks
**Moderate appetite.** Maximum acceptable residual risk rating: **High (score ≤ 15).**
- Critical operational risks require CRO + CISO joint escalation

### 3.4 Appetite Breach Protocol

When a risk's residual rating exceeds the appetite threshold for its category:
1. Risk Owner must notify the CRO within 5 business days
2. CRO presents the breach to the Risk Committee at the next quarterly meeting
3. Risk Committee approves a remediation plan with specific milestones and deadline
4. If not remediated within one quarter, the Risk Committee must either:
   a. Approve a formal exception with documented business justification, or
   b. Escalate to the Board for decision
5. Appetite breaches and remediation status are tracked in the risk register

## 4. Risk Register

### 4.1 Required Fields

Risk ID, description, category, likelihood, impact, inherent rating, residual rating, treatment strategy, controls, owner, review dates, appetite breach status.

### 4.2 Review Cadence

| Classification | Frequency |
|---------------|-----------|
| Critical | Monthly |
| High | Quarterly |
| Medium | Semi-annually |
| Low | Annually |

## 5. Risk Rating

5x5 matrix. Classifications: Low (1-4), Medium (5-9), High (10-15), Critical (16-25).

## 6. Risk Treatment

- Mitigate / Transfer / Accept / Avoid per standard framework
