## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the ERM framework for Cirrus Data Platforms Inc. in alignment with SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria CC3.1.

## 3. Risk Register

### 3.1 Maintenance

All risks tracked in Archer GRC. Required fields: risk ID, description, category, likelihood, impact, rating, treatment strategy, controls, owner, review dates, change history.

### 3.2 Review Cadence

| Classification | Frequency |
|---------------|-----------|
| Critical | Monthly |
| High | Quarterly |
| Medium | Semi-annually |
| Low | Annually |

## 4. Risk Appetite

- Information Security: Low appetite. Maximum residual: Medium (≤9).
- Compliance: Very low appetite. Maximum residual: Low (≤4).
- Operational: Moderate appetite. Maximum residual: High (≤15).

## 5. Risk Rating

5x5 matrix. Classifications: Low (1-4), Medium (5-9), High (10-15), Critical (16-25).

## 6. Emerging Risk Identification

### 6.1 Formal Process Requirements

Cirrus Data maintains a structured emerging risk identification process that includes:

1. **Quarterly Threat Intelligence Briefings:** Formal briefings prepared by the Threat Intelligence team using inputs from FS-ISAC, vendor advisories, and OSINT sources. Documented in the Threat Intelligence Report format.

2. **Technology Horizon Scanning:** Semi-annual assessment of emerging technologies, architectural changes, and new threat vectors relevant to Cirrus Data's platform. Conducted by the Security Architecture team and documented as a formal report.

3. **Regulatory Change Monitoring:** Monthly scan of regulatory developments by the Legal/Compliance team. New or amended regulations relevant to Cirrus Data are documented in the Regulatory Change Register.

4. **Industry Peer Benchmarking:** Annual participation in FS-ISAC industry risk survey and benchmarking exercise. Results compared against Cirrus Data's risk register.

### 6.2 Emerging Risk Assessment

When an emerging risk is identified through any of the above channels:
- A formal risk assessment must be completed within 30 days
- If the emerging risk is rated High or Critical, it must be added to the risk register within 10 business days
- The SGC must be notified of any newly identified emerging risks at the next quarterly meeting

### 6.3 Documentation

All emerging risk identification activities must be documented with:
- Source of the emerging risk signal
- Preliminary risk assessment (likelihood, impact, affected systems)
- Recommended treatment approach
- Timeline for formal risk register inclusion
