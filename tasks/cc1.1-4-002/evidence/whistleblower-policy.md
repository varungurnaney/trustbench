# Whistleblower and Ethics Reporting Policy

**Document ID:** POL-WB-001
**Version:** 1.4
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024
**Owner:** General Counsel
**Classification:** Internal
**Organization:** Crestwood Dynamics

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the mechanisms and protections for reporting suspected violations of law, regulation, company policy, or ethical standards at Crestwood Dynamics. The policy ensures that all personnel have access to confidential reporting channels and are protected from retaliation.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, interns, board members, and third-party partners of Crestwood Dynamics.

## 3. Reporting Channels

### 3.1 Internal Channels

Individuals may report concerns through:

- **Direct supervisor** — for routine policy questions or minor concerns
- **Human Resources** — for workplace conduct, harassment, or discrimination
- **Compliance team** — for regulatory or policy compliance concerns
- **General Counsel** — for legal or contractual matters
- **CISO** — for information security violations

### 3.2 Anonymous Ethics Hotline

Crestwood Dynamics maintains an anonymous ethics hotline operated by EthicsPoint, an independent third-party provider:

- **Phone:** 1-855-555-0147 (available 24/7)
- **Web:** https://crestwood.ethicspoint.com
- **Available in:** English, Spanish, French

Reports submitted through the hotline are received by the Ethics Committee, which includes the General Counsel, CHRO, and Chief Compliance Officer. No identifying information is required.

### 3.3 Board-Level Reporting

For concerns involving the CEO or executive leadership, reports may be submitted directly to the Board Audit Committee Chair via a dedicated email address provided during annual ethics training.

## 4. Investigation Process

### 4.1 Receipt and Triage

All reports are acknowledged within 2 business days. The Ethics Committee triages reports by severity:

- **Critical:** Fraud, theft, regulatory violations, safety hazards — investigated within 5 business days
- **High:** Policy violations, conflicts of interest, data breaches — investigated within 10 business days
- **Medium:** Process gaps, minor policy deviations — investigated within 30 business days

### 4.2 Investigation Standards

Investigations are conducted by:

- Internal Audit for financial matters
- HR for workplace conduct matters
- Legal for regulatory and contractual matters
- CISO for information security matters

External investigators may be engaged for matters involving senior management.

### 4.3 Resolution and Documentation

Investigation findings and outcomes are documented in the Ethics Case Management System. Summary statistics are reported to the Board Audit Committee quarterly.

## 5. Non-Retaliation

### 5.1 Protection Guarantee

Crestwood Dynamics strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who:

- Reports a concern in good faith through any available channel
- Participates in an investigation
- Refuses to participate in activities that violate policy or law

### 5.2 Consequences of Retaliation

Retaliation is treated as a separate, serious policy violation and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 5.3 Monitoring

HR monitors for patterns that could indicate retaliation, including adverse employment actions taken within 12 months of a report being filed.

## 6. Annual Acknowledgment

All employees must acknowledge this policy annually as part of the ethics and compliance training program. Acknowledgment rates are tracked and reported to the Ethics Committee.

## 7. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually by the General Counsel and approved by the Board Audit Committee.
