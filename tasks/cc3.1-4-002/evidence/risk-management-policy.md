## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the enterprise risk management framework for Nexus Financial Technologies Inc. in compliance with SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria CC3.1.

## 2. Risk Governance

### 2.1 Risk Committee

The Risk Committee meets quarterly to oversee the risk program:
- Nicole Tanaka, CISO (Chair)
- Andrew Park, CRO
- Elena Rodriguez, VP Engineering
- James Foster, General Counsel
- Rachel Wei, CFO

### 2.2 Risk Owners

Each identified risk must have a designated Risk Owner who is accountable for treatment implementation, monitoring, and reporting.

## 3. Risk Register

### 3.1 Maintenance

All risks tracked in ServiceNow GRC. Required fields:
- Risk ID, description, category
- Likelihood and impact (5x5)
- Risk classification
- Treatment strategy and plan
- Risk owner
- Review dates

### 3.2 Review Cadence

| Classification | Review Frequency |
|---------------|------------------|
| Critical | Monthly |
| High | Quarterly |
| Medium | Semi-annually |
| Low | Annually |

### 3.3 Risk Appetite

Nexus Financial maintains a **low** risk appetite for information security and regulatory risks. Specifically:
- Critical risks: must be treated immediately; acceptance requires Board resolution
- High risks: treatment plan required within 30 days; acceptance requires CISO + CRO joint approval
- Medium risks: treatment plan within 90 days
- Low risks: monitored; treatment optional

### 3.4 Risk Appetite Monitoring

When a risk's residual rating exceeds the defined appetite threshold, the Risk Owner must:
- Escalate to the CRO within 5 business days
- Present a remediation plan at the next Risk Committee meeting
- Document the remediation timeline and expected residual risk after treatment

## 4. Risk Treatment

- Mitigate: Implement controls
- Transfer: Insurance or contract
- Accept: Per appetite thresholds in Section 3.3
- Avoid: Eliminate the activity

## 5. Exception Process

Risk acceptance for Critical risks requires:
1. Formal exception request with business justification
2. Compensating controls documentation
3. CRO recommendation
4. CISO approval
5. Board of Directors resolution
6. Quarterly review of the exception
7. Maximum duration: 12 months
