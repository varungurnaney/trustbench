# Human Resources Security Policy

**Document ID:** POL-HR-003
**Version:** 1.4
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025
**Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Classification:** Internal
**Last Reviewed:** January 10, 2025

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements for personnel security practices at Meridian Analytics to ensure that employees and contractors are qualified, trustworthy, and equipped to fulfill their responsibilities in alignment with the organization's security objectives.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all full-time employees, part-time employees, temporary staff, and third-party contractors who access Meridian Analytics information systems or facilities.

## 3. Pre-Employment Screening

### 3.1 Identity Verification

All candidates must provide government-issued identification prior to the first day of employment. Identity documents are verified by the HR onboarding coordinator and copies are retained in the personnel file.

### 3.2 Reference Checks

Reference checks shall be performed for all candidates being considered for positions at Meridian Analytics. A minimum of two professional references must be contacted and documented.

### 3.3 Employment History Verification

HR shall verify the candidate's employment history for the preceding five years. Verification includes job title, dates of employment, and reason for departure where available.

## 4. Security Awareness Training

### 4.1 Initial Training

All new employees and contractors must complete the Security Awareness Training program within their first 30 days of employment. The training covers:

- Information security fundamentals
- Acceptable use of company resources
- Data classification and handling
- Incident reporting procedures
- Social engineering awareness
- Physical security requirements

### 4.2 Ongoing Training

Employees must complete security awareness training on an ongoing basis to remain current with evolving threats and organizational policy changes.

### 4.3 Training Content

Training materials are developed and maintained by the Information Security team in collaboration with HR. Content is reviewed when updates are deemed necessary.

## 5. Acceptable Use

### 5.1 Acceptable Use Policy Acknowledgment

All employees and contractors must read and sign the Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) prior to being granted access to company information systems. Signed acknowledgments are retained in the employee's personnel file.

### 5.2 AUP Scope

The AUP covers acceptable use of email, internet, mobile devices, removable media, cloud services, and social media as they relate to company information and systems.

## 6. Role Changes and Transfers

### 6.1 Access Review on Transfer

When an employee transfers to a new role or department, a review of their access privileges must be conducted. Access no longer required for the new role should be revoked.

### 6.2 Manager Responsibility

The receiving manager is responsible for initiating the access review request through the IT Service Desk.

## 7. Termination Procedures

### 7.1 Access Revocation

Upon termination, voluntary or involuntary, all logical and physical access must be revoked within 24 hours. This includes:

- Network and VPN access
- Application accounts
- Email access
- Badge and facility access
- Remote access tokens

### 7.2 Asset Return

All company assets must be returned on or before the last day of employment, including laptops, mobile devices, access badges, and any printed materials.

## 8. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| CHRO | Policy approval, escalation authority |
| HR Manager | Screening execution, training coordination |
| Hiring Managers | Reference checks, onboarding compliance |
| Information Security | Training content, access reviews |
| IT Operations | Access provisioning and revocation |

## 9. Compliance

Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. Contractors in violation may have their engagement terminated.

## 10. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually or upon significant organizational changes.
