## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for identifying, assessing, managing, and monitoring information security and operational risks across Vertex AI Corp. It ensures that risks are consistently evaluated and treated in alignment with the organization's risk appetite and SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria CC3.1.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all business units, departments, information systems, and third-party relationships within Vertex AI Corp. It covers:

- Information security risks
- Technology and infrastructure risks
- Operational risks
- Regulatory and compliance risks
- Third-party and vendor risks
- Business continuity risks

## 3. Risk Governance Structure

### 3.1 Security Governance Committee (SGC)

The Security Governance Committee is responsible for oversight of the risk management program. The SGC is composed of:

- CISO (Chair) -- Elena Vasquez
- Chief Risk Officer -- Rajesh Patel
- VP of Engineering -- Thomas Wright
- VP of Product -- Amara Osei
- General Counsel -- Patricia Holloway
- CFO -- Robert Tanaka

The SGC meets **quarterly** to:
- Review the risk register and risk ratings
- Approve risk treatment plans for High and Critical risks
- Review risk exceptions and acceptances
- Assess emerging risks and threat landscape changes

### 3.2 Risk Owners

Each identified risk must have a designated **Risk Owner** who is responsible for:
- Implementing the approved treatment plan
- Monitoring risk indicators
- Reporting status changes to the SGC
- Ensuring timely review of assigned risks

## 4. Risk Assessment Process

### 4.1 Annual Risk Assessment

A comprehensive risk assessment shall be conducted **annually**, typically in Q1. The assessment must:

- Cover all risk categories defined in Section 2
- Incorporate threat intelligence and industry benchmarks
- Include input from all business unit leaders
- Be documented in a formal Risk Assessment Report
- Be reviewed and approved by the SGC

### 4.2 Ad Hoc Risk Assessments

Additional risk assessments may be triggered by:
- Significant changes to the technology environment
- New regulatory requirements
- Security incidents or near-misses
- Mergers, acquisitions, or new business partnerships

## 5. Risk Rating Methodology

### 5.1 Likelihood Scale

| Rating | Level | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Rare | Unlikely to occur in the next 12 months |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur but not expected |
| 3 | Possible | Reasonable chance of occurrence |
| 4 | Likely | Expected to occur at least once |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected to occur multiple times |

### 5.2 Impact Scale

| Rating | Level | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Negligible | Minimal business impact, < $10K loss |
| 2 | Minor | Limited impact, $10K - $100K loss |
| 3 | Moderate | Moderate disruption, $100K - $500K loss |
| 4 | Major | Significant disruption, $500K - $2M loss |
| 5 | Catastrophic | Severe business impact, > $2M loss |

### 5.3 Risk Rating Matrix

Risk Rating = Likelihood x Impact

| Rating Range | Classification | Treatment Requirement |
|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1-4 | Low | Monitor and review annually |
| 5-9 | Medium | Treatment plan recommended |
| 10-15 | High | Treatment plan required, SGC review |
| 16-25 | Critical | Immediate treatment plan required, Board notification |

## 6. Risk Treatment Options

For each identified risk, one of the following treatment strategies must be selected:

1. **Mitigate** -- Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact
2. **Transfer** -- Transfer risk via insurance or contractual arrangements
3. **Accept** -- Formally accept the risk (requires CISO approval for High risks; CISO + Board approval for Critical risks)
4. **Avoid** -- Eliminate the activity or condition creating the risk

## 7. Risk Register

### 7.1 Risk Register Maintenance

All identified risks shall be recorded in the GRC tool (ServiceNow GRC module). Each risk entry must include:

- Unique risk identifier
- Risk description
- Risk category
- Likelihood and impact ratings
- Calculated risk score
- Treatment strategy and plan
- Risk owner
- Last review date
- Next review date

### 7.2 Risk Register Reviews

The risk register shall be reviewed **quarterly** by the SGC. Individual risk owners must review their assigned risks at least quarterly and update status in the GRC tool.

## 8. Risk Monitoring and Reporting

### 8.1 Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

KRIs shall be defined for Critical and High risks and monitored continuously or at defined intervals. KRI thresholds trigger escalation procedures when exceeded.

### 8.2 Reporting

- **Quarterly:** Risk dashboard presented to SGC
- **Annually:** Comprehensive risk assessment report to Board of Directors
- **Ad Hoc:** Immediate notification to CISO for newly identified Critical risks

## 9. Exception Process

Risk acceptance for Critical risks requires:
1. Formal exception request with business justification
2. Documentation of compensating controls
3. CISO approval
4. Board of Directors notification and approval
5. Quarterly review of the exception
6. Documented expiration date (maximum 12 months)

## 10. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually or upon significant organizational changes.
