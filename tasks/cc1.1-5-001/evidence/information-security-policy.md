# Information Security Policy

**Document ID:** POL-ISEC-005
**Version:** 4.2
**Effective Date:** November 1, 2024
**Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer
**Classification:** Internal
**Organization:** Redstone Capital Partners

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the governance framework for Redstone Capital Partners' information security program. It defines the organizational commitment to protecting client assets, maintaining regulatory compliance, and ensuring the integrity of our technology infrastructure.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party service providers of Redstone Capital Partners across all offices (New York, London, Singapore).

## 3. Governance Structure

### 3.1 Security Governance Committee

The Security Governance Committee (SGC) provides strategic oversight of the information security program. The committee meets quarterly and is chaired by the CEO.

### 3.2 CISO Authority and Independence

The CISO has the authority to:

- Approve and enforce security policies and standards
- Direct incident response and breach notification activities
- Approve or deny security exceptions up to 90 days
- Halt deployments or transactions that pose unacceptable security risk

The CISO reports to the Chief Technology Officer and has a quarterly reporting obligation to the Board Risk Committee.

### 3.3 Security Budget

The security budget is proposed by the CISO, reviewed by the CTO, and approved by the CFO as part of the technology budget. The CISO presents the security investment plan to the SGC annually for endorsement.

## 4. Policy Review and Governance

### 4.1 Review Cycle

All information security policies are reviewed on an annual basis. The review cycle follows the calendar year, with reviews due by December 31 of each year. Reviews may occur earlier if triggered by:

- Significant security incidents
- Regulatory changes
- Material organizational changes (mergers, acquisitions)
- Changes in threat landscape

### 4.2 Approval Authority

- Tier 1 policies: Approved by SGC
- Tier 2 standards: Approved by CISO
- Tier 3 procedures: Approved by security function managers

## 5. Security Controls

### 5.1 Access Management

- Principle of least privilege
- Multi-factor authentication for all production and cloud access
- Privileged access management (PAM) for administrative accounts
- Quarterly access certification by system owners

### 5.2 Data Protection

- Data classification: Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted
- Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3 preferred, TLS 1.2 minimum)
- Tokenization for payment card data
- Data loss prevention across email, web, and endpoint

### 5.3 Vulnerability and Patch Management

- Continuous vulnerability scanning
- Critical patches within 72 hours
- High patches within 14 days
- Quarterly penetration testing

### 5.4 Incident Response

- 24/7 SOC with Tier 1/2/3 escalation
- P1 incident: CISO notification within 30 minutes
- Breach notification per regulatory requirements (SEC, GDPR, MAS)
- Post-incident review within 5 business days

## 6. Compliance

This policy supports compliance with SEC Regulation S-P, GDPR, MAS TRM Guidelines, SOC 2, and ISO 27001.

## 7. Enforcement

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination, and in cases of criminal activity, referral to law enforcement.
