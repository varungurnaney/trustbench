# Physical Security Policy

**Document ID:** POL-PS-003
**Version:** 2.1
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2025
**Owner:** Facilities and Security Operations
**Classification:** Internal

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes Orion DataTech's requirements for physical security controls to protect personnel, facilities, equipment, and information assets from unauthorized physical access, damage, and interference.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all Orion DataTech facilities, including the corporate headquarters at 2200 Innovation Drive, Suite 400, Austin, TX, and the primary data center located at DataVault Colocation, 8500 Network Blvd, Dallas, TX.

## 3. Facility Access Zones

### 3.1 Zone Classification

All Orion DataTech facilities are divided into the following security zones:

| Zone | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Public | Areas accessible to the general public | Building lobby, parking garage |
| General Office | Standard work areas requiring badge access | Open office floors, conference rooms |
| Restricted | Areas containing sensitive systems or data | Server rooms, network closets, executive offices |
| High Security | Critical infrastructure areas | Data center cages, backup media storage |

### 3.2 Zone Access Requirements

Access to each zone requires progressively higher levels of authorization:

- **Public:** No badge required
- **General Office:** Valid employee badge
- **Restricted:** Employee badge with specific zone authorization approved by department head
- **High Security:** Employee badge with specific zone authorization approved by CISO and Facilities Director

## 4. Access Management

### 4.1 Badge Issuance and Lifecycle

All employees are issued a proximity badge upon hire. Badges are programmed by the Facilities team based on access authorization forms approved by the employee's manager. Badges are collected upon employee termination or reassignment.

### 4.2 Visitor Management

All visitors must sign in at the front reception desk and receive a temporary visitor badge. Visitor badges are clearly distinguishable from employee badges (orange lanyard vs. blue lanyard). Visitors must present government-issued photo identification, and the receptionist logs the visitor's name, company, host employee, and time of arrival. Visitor badges must be returned at reception upon departure.

### 4.3 Badge Deactivation

When an employee separates from the company, Human Resources notifies the Facilities team to deactivate the employee's badge. Deactivation should be completed promptly.

## 5. Surveillance

### 5.1 CCTV Coverage

CCTV cameras are installed at building entrances, server room doors, loading docks, and parking garage entry and exit points. Cameras operate 24/7 and footage is recorded to a network video recorder. Footage is retained in accordance with operational needs.

### 5.2 Monitoring

Security personnel monitor live CCTV feeds during business hours (7:00 AM to 7:00 PM). After-hours monitoring is performed via motion-activated alerts sent to the on-call security guard.

## 6. Environmental Controls

### 6.1 Fire Suppression

All server rooms and data center cages are equipped with FM-200 clean agent fire suppression systems. Fire suppression systems are tested annually.

### 6.2 HVAC

Server rooms maintain temperature between 64-75 degrees Fahrenheit with humidity between 40-60%. Environmental conditions are monitored via sensor arrays with automated alerts.

## 7. Data Center Physical Security

### 7.1 Colocation Provider

Orion DataTech's primary data center is hosted at DataVault Colocation. Physical security at the colocation facility is governed by DataVault's physical security controls, which are validated through their annual SOC 2 Type II report.

### 7.2 Cabinet Access

Orion DataTech cabinets at DataVault are secured with combination locks. Lock combinations are changed quarterly and distributed only to authorized Infrastructure team members.

## 8. Compliance and Review

### 8.1 Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually by the CISO and Facilities Director.

### 8.2 Compliance Auditing

Physical security controls are audited semi-annually. Audit findings are tracked to resolution.
